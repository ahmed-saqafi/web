Publications

Investigation of the Mechanical Properties of Repurposed-Net-Bag Reinforced Plastic

Saqafi, Ahmed A., Afnan Sadia, Al-Amin R. Tarek, Ahad A. Alim, Farzana Y. Tuly, and Md. A. Rahman

MIST International Journal of Science and Technology, 13(1), 59-64 (2025)

View Publication →

Abstract:

This study investigates the potential of recycling grocery net bags for reinforcing epoxy matrix composites, leveraging their favorable strength-to-weight ratio and offering a cost reduction and non-biodegradable waste reduction method. A composite material was made by combining 90 layers of fiber mesh with Lapox Metalam System B epoxy and hardener. The primary objective was to explore major mechanical properties, including ultimate tensile strength, yield strength, flexural strength, and impact strength. The ASTM-specified standards were maintained for the fabrication and testing of the specimen, utilizing a universal testing machine (UTM) and a pendulum impact tester. Test results show that elastic modulus of the composite ranges from 0.72 to 0.88 GPa and shear modulus ranges from 1.73 to 2.26 GPa. Also, the impact tests show an average impact strength of 66.27 kJ/m2.

Design and Development of a Universal Robot-Assisted Rehabilitation System for Musculoskeletal Physiotherapy

April 2024 - May 2025

Developed a lightweight, 3D-printed elbow-assist device with a compact cycloidal gearbox and cable-driven mechanism for precise, low-cost physiotherapy. The system is capable of smooth flexion–extension with accuracy and consistent performance across varying loads and speeds. The device provides an affordable, lightweight, and portable rehabilitation solution that can improve patient recovery and accessibility to physiotherapy.

Courses Taught

ME 376 – Control Engineering Sessional

This course provides hands-on experience in analyzing, modeling, and controlling various physical systems, including mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, and pneumatic. Students will learn to apply control engineering principles in real-world scenarios through laboratory experiments that bridge theory and practice. The curriculum covers block diagrams, transfer functions, and the use of PID controllers. A significant portion of the course involves system response analysis and simulation, primarily using MATLAB, along with the design and performance evaluation of feedback and digital/robust control systems. The main objective is to develop practical skills with modern control tools and simulation software.

ME 142 – Workshop Sessional

This course offers practical training in fundamental manufacturing processes. Students gain hands-on experience with machining, welding, casting, molding, and sheet metal working. They learn to use key workshop tools, including lathes, milling machines, and drilling machines, while also practicing essential skills like measuring, marking, and cutting. The primary goal is to introduce students to these processes and help them develop the practical skills needed for fabricating simple mechanical components. The course also emphasizes the importance of safety and quality in manufacturing practices.

ME 180 – Basic Engineering Drawing

ME 150 – Mechanical Engineering Drawing

This course focuses on both manual and CAD-based engineering drawing. Students learn to communicate technical information accurately by creating and interpreting engineering sketches and drawings. The content includes orthographic projection, isometric and sectional views, and the fundamentals of 2D drafting using SolidWorks or similar CAD software. The objective is to enable students to read, modify, and create technical drawings, laying a solid foundation for future design and drafting courses.
