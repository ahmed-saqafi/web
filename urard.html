Autonomous Rover Navigation System

A cutting-edge autonomous rover platform developed to navigate complex terrains using real-time sensor fusion, machine learning, and robust control algorithms.

Impact

The project advances autonomous vehicle research by offering a scalable test platform for navigation in unstructured environments. It demonstrates applications in disaster response, planetary exploration, and precision agriculture.

Design & Analysis

The system integrates LiDAR, GPS, IMUs, and stereo vision into a unified sensor fusion pipeline. A modular chassis design enables rapid prototyping, while CAD and FEA simulations ensured mechanical integrity under diverse load conditions.

Evaluation

Performance was validated through field trials over varying terrains, with a 92% navigation accuracy and consistent obstacle avoidance at speeds up to 3 m/s. Energy efficiency improved by 18% compared to baseline designs.

Recognition

The project received the Best Innovation Award at the National Robotics Challenge and was presented at the IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA).
